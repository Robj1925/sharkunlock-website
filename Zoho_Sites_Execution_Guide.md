## 🚪 Stage 1: Logging In & Launching the Zoho Sites Site Builder
*Goal: Navigate from a blank browser window into your live website editing workspace.*

1.  Open your web browser (Google Chrome or Safari are recommended) and type **`https://one.zoho.com`** into the address bar at the top, then press Enter.
2.  On the sign-in screen, enter your admin email address, click **Next**, enter your password, and click **Sign In**.
3.  You will land on the **Zoho One Admin Console (Dashboard)**. This screen displays a grid of all active application icons.
4.  In the search bar at the top center of the screen, type the word: `Sites`.
5.  Locate the **Sites app icon** (which looks like a blue globe or circular grid with the word "Sites" underneath). Click this icon.
6.  This will open the **Zoho Sites Console** in a new browser tab.
    *   *Note:* If you land directly on the **Zoho Sites Dashboard Console** (which shows your traffic chart, space occupied, and an "Edit Site" button in the top right), skip directly to **Step 8**.
7.  If you are on the list of sites under "My Sites", find the card representing **`sharkunlock.com`**. Hover your mouse cursor over this card and click the button labeled **"Access Site"** (or click the site name/thumbnail).
8.  You are now on the **Zoho Sites Dashboard Console (ZCMS)**, which is the management panel for your site (matching the screenshot you provided):
    *   **The Left Sidebar Menu:** A vertical dark blue menu column displaying options such as **Dashboard**, **Pages**, **Files**, **Blog**, **Forms**, etc.
    *   **The Central Panel:** Displays your site name **"Shark Unlock Interlock"**, its URL (`www.sharkunlock.com`), a thumbnail preview of the site, a **"Traffic in last 7 days"** chart, and storage/page details (e.g., 20/50 pages).
    *   **The Top-Right Action Buttons:** Located in the top-right corner of the window, you will see a grey **"Edit Site"** button (with a pencil icon) next to a green **"Publish"** button.
9.  To enter the actual visual page layout editor, click the grey **"Edit Site"** button (with the pencil icon next to it) in the top-right corner of this screen.
10. This will load the live **Zoho Sites ZCMS Editor Canvas** in your browser (matching the screenshot you provided). Take 10 seconds to familiarize yourself with this actual builder workspace layout:
    *   **The Persistent Left ZCMS Sidebar:** The dark blue vertical column on the far-left remains. You can use it to access **Pages** (to add or clone pages), **Files** (to manage uploads), and **Blog** (to configure articles).
    *   **The Top-Left Add Button:** The bright blue `+` (plus) button positioned in the top-left corner (next to the "Sites" logo). Clicking this button opens the widgets/sections pop-out tray to add headings, text, buttons, grid sections, and HTML code snippets to your pages.

    *   **The Right Floating Toolbar:** A small, white vertical strip floating along the right edge of the screen. It contains a paint palette icon with a gear (Design/Theme customization) and a pencil-on-document icon (visual page styles helper).
    *   **The Central Canvas:** The main body of your screen showing your live website homepage, where you can hover and click to edit individual text or image elements directly.

## 🎨 Stage 2: Accessing the Visual Style Editor & Global Themes Settings
*Goal: Establish our premium slate-navy, crisp white, and burnt orange brand identity across all pages.*

### Step 1: Navigating to the Visual Editor and Presets Menu
1.  Look at the standard Site Builder editing canvas.
2.  Locate the **Right Floating Toolbar** (the white vertical strip floating on the right side of the canvas).
3.  Click the **Visual Editor/Design icon** (which looks like a circular artist's paint palette with a gear in the middle).
4.  Your screen will transition into the **Visual Editor view**. A sidebar panel titled **"Customization"** will open on the **right side** of the screen (matching your screenshot).
5.  At the very top of this right sidebar list, locate the option labeled **"Presets"**. Click this **"Presets"** option.

### Step 2: Setting up your Color Palette
1.  Under the expanded **Presets** menu in the right sidebar, click **"Custom Values"** (or click the active color scheme's edit button).
2.  You will see a list of individual theme color variables (e.g. *Colour Uno, Colour Duo, Colour Trio*, etc.). Click on the hex value box for **Colour Uno** (Primary Color) to open the color selection popup.
3.  At the bottom of the color selection popup, double-click the HASH text input field (starts with `#`) and type: `0f172a` (This is your Slate Navy structural color). Press Enter.
4.  Click on the hex value box for **Colour Duo** (Secondary Color / Accent), double-click the HASH text input, and type: `ea580c` (This is your Burnt Orange accent color). Press Enter.
5.  Click on the hex value box for **Colour Trio** (Background Color), double-click the HASH text input, and type: `f8fafc` (This is your Soft Off-White structural color). Press Enter.
6.  Click **"Save"** or **"Apply"** at the bottom of the colors picker window.

### Step 3: Setting up your Fonts
1.  While still inside the **Presets** menu (or click the back arrow **`<`** at the top left of the sidebar to return to the main Presets options), look for the **Typography / Fonts** settings.
2.  Locate the two primary font selector dropdowns: **Base Font** and **Heading Font**:
    *   Click the **Heading Font** drop-down, search for `Inter`, and click it. Under weight settings, select **Bold** or **700**.
    *   Click the **Base Font (Body)** drop-down, search for `Open Sans`, and click **Open Sans** to apply it.
3.  Click the blue **"Save"** button at the bottom-right of the fonts panel.

### Step 4: Exiting the Visual Editor
1.  Once your presets for colors and fonts are applied and saved, look at the very top-right corner of your browser screen (on the dark blue header bar next to the URL).
2.  Click the white **"X"** close button. This will exit the Visual Editor and return you to the standard Site Builder canvas to begin constructing your pages.
